The Story of Billie

Christmas Traditions

Have you ever wondered why we do the things that we do during Christmas? Well, let me tell you, A little wiener dog named Billie certainly has. He has always been curious about the Christmas traditions. He was woken early on Christmas Eve morning by a weird sound. He looked up and saw the ghost of Christmas past, which happened to be his old best friend Charlie.

Charlie took Billie all through the past and explained all of Christmas traditions. The first thing they did was go outside, which is where he explained the meaning of a snowflake, the white of the snowflake represents joy, peace, calmness, & purity.

After that, they went back to the house and there they saw a gingerbread house, Billie asked why the gingerbread house was a Christmas tradition. Charlie then proceeded to explain that the first gingerbread recipe came from Grease in 2400 B.C and that Chinese recipes developed in the 10th century, after this Europeans developed their recipe and they decided to make them into shapes. Queen Elizabeth was the first one to come up with the idea of decorating the gingerbread cookies.

The last tradition that they looked at was the Christmas tree, the first Christmas tree was brought into a home in the 16th century, the first tree documented to be brought into a house was in between the cities Estonia and Lithia. The first Christmas trees were hung upside down.

After Charlie explained all of the most common Christmas tradition to Billie, Billie woke up very confused as to what just happened. He ran downstairs to tell his family what he just learned, and then he remembered it was Christmas! He became ecstatic with joy!

The End!
